Between 17 and 21 June 2024, the ESEE-Growth 2024 International Conference will take place in the city of Pontevedra, an important event that will jointly host the 10th International Degrowth Conference and the 15th Conference of the European Society for Ecological Economics (ESEE). Under the title Science, Technology, and Innovation beyond growth: Cultivating collective creativity for a sustainable future, this event will be one of the most important events of the year at the University of Vigo, as it will bring together leading international experts in the field of degrowth and ecological economics to debate on highly relevant and topical issues such as energy, welfare, technology, political ecology, feminism, social justice, and many others.

In addition to the academic activities, the Conference will have a full agenda of extra-academic activities open to the public, including talks, round tables, workshops, and concerts, with the participation of communicators, writers, artists, activists, publishers...

The organisation of this event is being coordinated by the Post-Growth Innovation Lab, a research group of the University of Vigo directed by the researcher Mario Pansera. Although the whole group is involved in the organisation of this event, for everything to go perfectly, we will need more people during the days of the conference who can support the organising team in different tasks.   • Collaborate in the preparation of the spaces in which the activities will be held, and in the signposting of the same.
  • Preparing the rooms for the different sessions and activities.
  • Welcoming participants and assisting in the registration process.
  • Preparing materials to be given to participants
  • Providing logistical support for coffee breaks and catering services.
  • Informing and guiding participants through the event venues
  • Providing support in questions related to IT services to the speakers/presenters of the sessions.
  • Collaborate in dissemination and diffusion tasks before and during the Conference.
  • Collaborate in taking photographs and videos during the conference for the website and social networks.
notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- What will volunteers get in return? slug: "what-will-volunteers-get-in-return" position: 2 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

The most important is that it will allow them to participate for free in an international scientific event and to do so in an active and totally immersive way, by being part of the organisation of the event. But, in addition, they will have the following advantages:

  • When they are not performing the tasks assigned to them, volunteersÂ will be able to access all the activities of the Conference free of charge, without having to pay any registration fee.
  • At the end of the event, they will receive a document accrediting their participation in the Local Organising Committee of the conference (this type of merit is useful in public calls for human resources and is highly valued when it comes to accessing certain jobs).
  • They will also receive a certificate of attendance at the Conference, similar to what the other participants will receive.
  • 1.3 ECTS for University of Vigo students who participate in the event, both as paying attendees and as part of the volunteer team.
  • They will have free access to the official meals of the Conference during those days.
  • They will receive a t-shirt with an exclusive design created for the conference, which they will wear while doing volunteer work and keep as a souvenir at the end of the event.
  • They will be able to meet new people and be part of an international team.

Although the event will officially start on the afternoon of Tuesday 18 June (the opening day) activities will begin on Monday 17 June, with a PhD Symposium, the International Degrowth Network (IDN) Assembly, and several non-academic activities taking place in the afternoon. The Conference will end on Friday 21 June.

The estimated timetable for the collaboration of volunteers will be as follows:

  • Day 1 - Explanatory session by the organising team, team-building, and networking: date to be determined during the first half of June. It will be in hybrid modality (in-person and online)
  • Day 2 - Assignment of tasks and start of the preparation of spaces: Friday 14 June. In-person modality (for those already in the city)
  • Days 3-7 -Â Execution of tasks during the Conference: 17-21 JuneÂ 

Different groups will be organised and tasks will be distributed in such a way that each volunteer does not work for more than 5 hours a day and that all participants have the opportunity to attend the different sessions and activities of the Conference that may be of interest to them. The first shifts will start around 8.30 in the morning and the last will end around 21.30 in the evening. The volunteers' preferences and constraints will be considered when drawing up the timetables.

Anyone of legal age can apply to join the team of volunteers. It is important, however, that those who apply are willing to make the commitment and fulfill the tasksÂ assignedÂ by the Conference organising team.

Participation in this activity will be a great experience for the volunteers and, as indicated in the previous section, the organisers will try to adapt the tasks and schedules to the needs and conditions of each volunteer, and to ensure that they can also enjoy the Conference activities as assistants, limiting the time spent working at the event. But for this to happen, it is necessary that the people who form part of the volunteer team do so responsibly and that, if they offer to participate, they are honest with their commitment.
