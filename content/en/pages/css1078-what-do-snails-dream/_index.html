Degrowth futures require fundamentally different systems of provisioning, consumption, and sociality. Creative thinking skills are crucial toward this reconfiguring process, but have been systematically undermined by the modern world and its homogenization of values. A pervasive realism bounds conversations to merely ameliorating capitalism’s consequences such that broader economic visioning is precluded. We seek not to build an equally homogenous blueprint for a Degrowth future, but to celebrate and strengthen the capacity for group creativity as Degrowth imaginaries may still be bound by a creativity crisis of current systems. We are thus proposing an Interactive Contribution to collectively conceive new ideas for social configurations, and to digest wild imaginaries in a fun and unconventional activity. Our goal is to nurture the Degrowth pluriverse, celebrating methodologies that evoke divergent thinking and collective imagination.

This Interactive Contribution employs constraint-based brainstorming (Gould et al., 2023) to creatively co-imagine Degrowth futures and potential paths to achieve these. As Gould et al. explain, “Although it may seem that constraints would stifle expansive thinking, in fact, it is often the blank slate of nearly limitless possibilities that can lead to idea paralysis.” As such, this facilitation technique makes use of outlandish constraints in tandem with a problem-solving prompt to enable the iterative generation of ideas in a fun and engaging way. We begin with a challenge for the group to brainstorm, such as the structures of a post-growth transportation industry or community responses to mental health crises. The specific challenge will be provided at the event so that it is relatively novel for participants. For about 10 minutes, facilitators will announce a series of prompts that constrain the challenge in some absurd or extreme way, e.g. the solutions cannot involve speaking, or must be something a six-year-old would design. We intentionally craft these prompts to encourage new ideas that participants might otherwise not have considered or felt comfortable suggesting. Participants will pair off, develop a new idea, share it with their partner, listen to their partner’s idea, and then switch partners. The timing is fast, to keep participants active and facilitate radical creativity.

A second, longer phase promotes the contemplation and synthesis of disparate ideas into thematic elements of future imaginaries. Participants will spend several minutes reading and considering the ideas that have been generated, and then gather into groups to discuss their insights. Groups will spend about 10 minutes discussing their takeaways among themselves, and then 10-15 minutes in a broader discussion with all of the participants. These discussions will involve emotional and intellectual reactions to various ideas, the similarity and dissonances between ideas, and the takeaways that participants have based on the activity. Group discussion allows ideas to snowball into new, more coherent visions, thus mirroring the divergent phase that began the activity.

Overall, this approach encourages a playful collaborative environment to experiment with ideas that break the status quo, liberating radical imagination. The process, ideas, and takeaways will be recorded to be disseminated to wider audiences and all session participants will share co-authorship. Our goals include the popularization and experimentation of plural methodologies for collective imagination, and the co-creation of Degrowth futures.
