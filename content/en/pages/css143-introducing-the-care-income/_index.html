The GWS is an international movement based at the Crossroads Women’s Center in London (UK) and composed by a number of autonomous organizations in different countries. Started in 1972 as International Wages for Housework campaign, since 2020 the movement has actively contributed to shape the Care Income policy proposal, and started a number of related campaigns, also in collaboration with Extinction Rebellion UK. In 2021, Global Women’s Strike supported the massive year-long farmers’ & farmworkers’ protest against the three farm laws aimed at handing over agriculture to corporations. In the process, they established a partnership with a remarkable women’s movement organized in Self-Help Groups which was leading a transformation to Natural Farming in the State of Andhra Pradesh. As stated in the GWS’s website:

As mothers, carers, farmers/farm workers, land and human rights’ defenders, women do most of the work of feeding (starting with breastfeeding) and protecting families and communities, the soil and the environment. For doing this fundamental caring work of society, unwaged and low-waged, we are impoverished and denied basic rights, including to the land we work. Organising in self-help groups, women in Andhra Pradesh, India, are leading a massive community movement of natural farmers who regenerate the soil to grow healthy food and stop climate change. Scaling up internationally is crucial. A care income for all natural farmers would empower such movements, encourage all genders to contribute and to refuse work that pollutes. The subsidies now paid to poison soil, water and air, must be redirected to caring for people and the planet, starting in the Global South and working-class communities everywhere, especially Indigenous and other people of colour, who are hardest hit by the climate emergency.

Session agenda:

First part:

  • Introduction to the session and speakers: Stefania Barca (University of Santiago de C., FADA), “The Care Income from a feminist degrowth perspective – a persona appraisal” (10 minutes)
  • First speaker: Selma James (Global Women’s Strike), “From Wages for Housework to a Care Income for People and Planet: ﬁtiy years of sex-race-and-class struggles” (15 min)
  • Second speaker: Nina López (Global Women’s Strike), “Care Income campaigns around the world: victories, challenges, and alliance building” (15 minutes)
  • Respondents (tbc, 15 minutes) Interactive session (35 minutes)

Second part: Caring for the land and for ourselves: women’s leadership in community managed natural farming

This session will feature an invited speaker from Andra Pradesh (India), who will oﬀer a detailed presentation (30 minutes), followed by a respondent from the women’s group of Galicia’s peasant organization SLG (Sindicato Labrego Galego) (10 minutes), after which we will open the ﬂoor for a general discussion with the participants. We will ask people to register in advance and select about 20 participants among people with a background in farming practice and/or organizing.

The session will be moderated by Bibiana Martinez.
