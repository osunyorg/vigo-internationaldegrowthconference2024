During registration, each person will receive a mug made by local craft makers. We encourage you to use it for coffee and drinks throughout the conference to reduce the need for extra crockery, saving energy, water and soap. Once the conference ends, you can take it with you as a souvenir from Galicia.


The pottery of Buño (Olería de Buño) is one of the traditional centers of ceramics in the northwest of the Iberian Peninsula. In general, Buño pottery is of very good quality and highly valued compared to that of other centers of production. In Buño (Malpica - Galicia) pottery activity has been documented since the sixteenth century. The potter's trade is recorded as a traditional occupation from 1510. Along its history, most of the inhabitants of Buño have been related to the clay industry. At the end of the 20th century, the elders of the town told that there were more than eighty active potteries, with two wheel lathes in each pottery, one for the owner and another one for an official worker. The oleiros of Buño made utensils for daily use (pots, plates or jugs, among others). With the arrival of materials such as plastic, the pottery industry went into decline, causing most of the artisans to look for alternative employment.  At the end of the 20th century, the recovery of this sector began, turning it into a cultural and economic claim within Galicia. The industry was modernized and specialized as a craft that would find a market in tourism and souvenirs.  This updating and reconversion to manufacture decorative and artistic elements did not leave aside the manufacture of traditional pieces, and contributed to mix the knowledge of several generations while innovation came to stay.

Winner of awards and in search of continuous improvement, he has incorporated 3D printing and modeling without losing the essence of handmade products. At Alfarería Lista they make products that promote collaborative work and sustainability through design. In their pottery they respect the working techniques and the time it takes to deliver their products, understanding what it takes to produce something handmade. They manufacture their own handmade ceramic products, with more than 40 years of experience in the market. Its creative line has led to achieve the seal of Commitment to Quality Tourism. More than 45 years in the sector (since 1979).

The pottery tradition of Buño is still very much alive in the hands of young artisans like Alberto Añón (artisan of these mugs) fourth generation of a family saga of reference. Winner of awards and in search of continuous improvement, he has incorporated 3D printing and modeling without losing the essence of handmade products.

At Alfarería Lista they make products that promote collaborative work and sustainability through design. In their pottery they respect the working techniques and the time it takes to deliver their products, understanding what it takes to produce something handmade.

They manufacture their own handmade ceramic products, with more than 40 years of experience in the market. Its creative line has led to achieve the seal of Commitment to Quality Tourism. More than 45 years in the sector (since 1979).
