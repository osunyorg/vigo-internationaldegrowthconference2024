Are you addicted to growth? What does it actually mean to be addicted to growth? And why is this

important? The poster campaign "Addicted to Growth?" on the streets of Pontevedra sparks

precisely these questions, preceding the annual conference on degrowth, which is hosted in

Pontevedra this year. The campaign is inspired by the book "Addicted to Growth, Societal Therapy

for a Sustainable Wellbeing Future" by Robert Costanza.

We know what it means to be addicted to alcohol, or smoking: you are craving for a drink or a

cigarette and you can't hold yourself back, even when you know it is not good for you. You're not in

control anymore. But how does that work with (economic) growth? At the level of society? We refer

to the aforementioned book by Costanza for details, here we ouline the idea.

Most of us will agree that our current economic activty is eventually destroying our habitat, much

like what happened to Easter Island. We contend that the economy as a whole is the sum of our

individual behaviours. And if our society is addicted to growth then this is because each of us is

addicted to growth. For this reason the campaign focusses on the question: what does it mean to be

addicted to growth? On a personal level.

The answer to this question is not that hard. We may not have phrased it as addictive behaviour yet,

but we all know the answer. Addicted to growth means that you do things knowing, or at least

suspecting, that you should not do this in view of it's impact on our planet, the future and us. So let

us establish the way out:

Once you have realised that you are addicted to growth and you want to quit your addiction, we also

know how to do just that: envision your future life free of that addiction. So now we walk you

through the questions on the posters hoping to inspire you to form your vision of your future free

form your addiction to growth.
