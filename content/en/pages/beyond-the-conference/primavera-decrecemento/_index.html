The Degrowth Spring is a series of talks and workshops aimed at citizens organised within the framework of the ESEE-Growth 2024 International Conference, which will be held during the months of April, May, and June in the city of Pontevedra. It is promoted by the Post-Growth Innovation Lab group with the support of the University of Vigo and the Pontevedra City Council and counts on the collaboration and participation of different Galician and national people and organisations. 

An activity will be organised every week in which different topics of social interest such as work, housing, the banking system, or environmentalism will be discussed and debated from the perspective of post-growth/degrowth.

The events will take place in different locations in the city centre, and admission will be free until full capacity is reached.

Workshop "Returning to the roots, returning to growth" position: 0 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • When:
    Thursday 11 April, 6.30 p. m.
  • Where:
    Casa das Campás (Don Filiberto St., Pontevedra)

  • Who:
    Rosa María Regueiro & Xosé Ramón Doldán (University of Santiago de Compostela)

Talk/workshop to look at the changes in the use of resources in objects-services of great importance in our daily lives. We will work with two goods, one more modern and the other older, which would have the same function, and we will analyse them from an economic, environmental, ecological, energetic, technical, social point of view... advantages and disadvantages, costs, consumption of materials, impact on the environment. For example, a current electric car versus a car from 20 years ago; the tram versus another means of transport; the cooling of water in containers versus plastic, etc.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Thursday, 18 April - Talk "Could anybody live here? - Access to housing and degrowth" position: 1 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • When:
    Thursday 18 April, at 7.00 p. m.
  • Where:
    Nasa (Travesía da Galera, Pontevedra) 

  • Who:
    Juan Rodríguez Medela & the Sindicato de Inquilinas de Vigo-Tui-Baixo Miño
    Moderated by: Víctor Sánchez & Alejandro Fortuny (Post-Growth Innovation Lab)

We will raise the current scenario of access to housing in Pontevedra and will help imagine alternatives where economic growth and financial speculation are not the logic that dictates access to housing. To this end, we will count on Juan Medela, an anthropologist living in Vigo who studied the urban space of Granada and formed part of the anthropological studies group La Corrala, together with the Sindicato de Inquilinas de Vigo as a social movement that imagines other ways of inhabiting.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Thursday, 25 April - Talk "Ethical Finance. Much more than sustainable products" position: 2 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • When:
    Thursday April 25, at 7.00 p. m.
  • Where:
    Pazo Mugartegui (Praza da Pedreira, Pontevedra) 

  • Who:
    Diego Domínguez Carrasco (Fiare Banca Ética)
    Presenter: Alejandro Fortuny (Post-Growth Innovation Lab)

Work is a fundamental right that contributes to our personal dignity and allows us to acquire an economic capacity, making us consumers. People have the capacity to be interested in the design of a product and, in conscience, we can choose. But are we aware of our financial consumption?

Finance has become a fundamental axis of our society today, and there is little left that we do not measure in euros. In this scenario, financial institutions are the key players. We leave our savings with them, and we ask them for support. We are consuming more and more finances, but with what objective? Do we seek to grow in purchasing power? Do we want to grow in our possibilities to develop future projects? What impact do our financial decisions have? Can we consume finances and grow in collective well-being?

Ethical Finance offers us an opportunity for transformation, for a paradigm shift. It is not about selling sustainable products, it is about developing sustainable projects, changing finance, and doing finance differently. How far is this possible?

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Thursday 16 May - Talk "Notebooks vs. Tablets - Should we de-digitalize education?" position: 5 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • When:
    Thursday 16 May, at 7.00 p. m.

  • Where:
    Casa das Campás (Don Filiberto St., Pontevedra)

  • Who:
    Ecoloxistas en Acción Pontevedra
    Presenter: Víctor Sánchez (Post-Growth Innovation Lab)

Ecoloxistas en Acción is organising a campaign among educational centres for the de-digitalisation of education. Their criticism is based on the ecological impact of extracting raw materials to create new electronic devices. Bringing these debates together with alternative ways of relating to technology and education is the aim of this activity, in which two questions will be addressed:

  1. The main criticisms collected in this campaign will be presented
  2. The alternatives will be presented: What would a de-digitalised school look like?
notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Thursday 23 May - Talk "Ecology versus the economy or ecology versus extractivism? Local struggles in Galicia against large industries" position: 6 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • When:
    Thursday 23 May, at 7.00 p. m.

  • Where:
    Space to be determined

  • Who:
    Antón Masa (Asociación Pola Defensa da Ría), Isabel García (Mina Touro-O Pino Non), Patricia Gamallo (Eólicos, Así Non)
    Moderated by: Gonzalo Rodríguez Rodríguez (University of Santiago de Compostela)

Conversation between Galician local organizations that fight against industrial activities of high environmental impact in their territories. We will address the false dilemma between economy and ecology. This self-serving dichotomy that places the protection of the ecological system against economic activities and employment hides the fact that without ecology there can be no economy, not even extractive industries. This conversation reveals the real dichotomy between the ecological heritage and the extractivist industrial development model. The discrepancy is therefore established between economic models, since a macro-industry with concentrated ownership and enormous environmental impact, which crushes and degrades the environment, impoverishes politics, and polarizes society, is not the same as a fabric of economic organizations of moderate size, plural, resilient, convivial and respectful of the socio-ecological system. We will learn about the struggles of each group, their values, and their alternative proposals for a dignified economic model. We will also reflect on what common factors there are in their struggles and how they can help each other.

  • When:
    Thursday 30 April, at 7.00 p. m.

  • Where:
    Casa das Campás (Don Filiberto St., Pontevedra)

  • Who:
    Pablo Casares Balsa (MeteoGalicia Atmosphere Technician)
    Moderated by: Begoña de Bernardo Miño (membro da Xunta Directiva da Rede para o Decrecemento EO-Navia, Galiza, O Bierzo e Presidenta de Véspera de Nada)

Pablo Casares will explain what the IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) is and the most optimistic and pessimistic forecasts that this intergovernmental panel foresees at a global level. He will then talk about the possible climate scenarios that could occur in Galicia (Mediterraneanisation of the climate, drop in temperatures, appearance of cyclones in the Atlantic, reduction of rainfall, increase in drought and snow days...).

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Thursday 13 June - Round Table "Environmental organisations in communicating the limits to growth" position: 9 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • When:
    Thursday 13 June, at 7.00 p. m.

  • Where:
    Museo Provincial (Padre Amoedo Carballo St. 3, Pontevedra)

  • Who:
    Eva Saldaña (Greenpeace), Luis González Reyes (Ecologistas en Acción), Inés Entenza Sequeiros (Juventud por el Clima), Olga García Moreno (Rebelión Científica) e Jorge Zhou (Extinction Rebellion)
    Moderated by: David Soto (Post-Growth Innovation Lab)

Material growth is currently at the heart of our society's individual and collective aspirations. The existence of ecological limits to material growth necessarily truncates these aspirations. As a consequence, discourses highlighting the limits to growth and the need for urgent action are widely rejected by large segments of society. Environmental organisations are therefore faced with multiple dilemmas in their communication strategy. In this roundtable, representatives of some of the most important long-standing and new organisations will share their experiences with us.

  • When:
    Firay 13 June, at 7.00 p. m.

  • Where:
    Ramal Port, Vilagarcía de Arousa
  • Organiser: 
    Post-Growth Innovation Lab & Greenpeace

We will visit the iconic Greenpeace icebreaker Arctic Sunrise, which will dock for a few days in Vilagarcía de Arousa (Peirao do Ramal). The Arctic Sunrise arrives in this town as part of the organisation's expedition to demand the ratification of the Global Oceans Treaty and the creation of marine protected areas. Greenpeace will offer us a guided tour where crew and activists will tell us about the struggles experienced by this ship in favour of a healthy and peaceful planet. 

If you want to come by public transport with us, we will leave Pontevedra Station on the 6:32 p. m. train towards Vilagarcía de Arousa and from there we will walk to Peirao do Ramal. We will return on the 10:12 p. m. train (remember to buy your tickets well in advance). If you go on your own, we will meet at 7:00 p. m. in front of the boat.
