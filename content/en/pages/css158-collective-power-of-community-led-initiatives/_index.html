Harnessing the collective power of community-led initiatives as enablers of a post-growth European Green Deal summary: >-

How can an ambitious Post-Growth European Green Deal be realised through community-led action across Europe? ECOLISE, the European Network for Community-led Initiatives on Climate Change and sustainability, and its partners, have formulated key ideas and policy demands for transformative change towards a good life for all within planetary boundaries in the “Time for Collective Action Manifesto”. As a supporting partner of the Beyond Growth conference that took place in May 2023 in the European Parliament, ECOLISE endorses the call of +400 Civil Society organisations and experts for a Post-Growth Europe. The challenge is immense: the overall aim is to fix a broken system based on infinite economic growth, hyper-individualisation and over-consumption. A sufficiency-based approach to addressing the climate crisis is needed, not least as the IPCC (2022) states that with the right policies, infrastructures and technology to enable fundamental lifestyle and behaviour change, up to 70% of global greenhouse gas emissions could be reduced by 2050. This manifesto is a call-to-action to EU policy makers, as well as non-governmental organisations, civil society organisations and community-led initiatives, to foster transformative community-led development across Europe. The purpose of this session is to introduce participants to the work of ECOLISE and its action programme “Communities for Futue” explore synergies with Degrowth, and facilitate a discussion on how to effectively promote the objectives of a Post-Growth European Green Deal with community-led initiatives at the heart. In the beginning, the organisers will provide an introduction to ECOLISE, including its community-led approach and its relationship with Degrowth This will be followed by an interactive session on policy recommendations that promote transformative, community-led local development in Europe. Through ECOLISE’s engagement in advocacy at EU levels, it possesses critical experience and partnerships to work towards realising a post-growth policy agenda at EU levels. This transdisciplinary knowledge is to be brought into a fruitful exchange with findings from research on viable degrowth policies.

Presenters

  • Amelie Krug, ECOLISE
  • Juan del Rio, ECOLISE

1. Introduction to ECOLISE

ECOLISE, as European Network of community-led initiatives on climate change and sustainability will be shortly introduced along the following lines:

Who are the members? What are its mission and values? Which are key activities of ECOLISE? Which synergies exist with the Degrowth movement?

2. Interactive Session: Shaping a Post-Growth European Green Deal with community-led initiatives at the heart2.1 ECOLISE’s policy positioning on the European Green Deal

 ECOLISE and partners advocacy campaign on the “Time for Collective Action Manifesto”:

  •  Timeline and Milestones of the advocacy campaign
  • Contextual information on consultation process which involved with a wide range of key CSOs in the domains of Social Solidarity Economy, Climate Change and Rural Development
  • Presentation of key messages and demands
  • Overview of campaign events and activities 

2.2. Why Community-led initiatives are enablers of a Post-Growth European Green Deal

In this part of the session, participants will discuss, if and how community-led initiatives can contribute to realising a Post-growth European Green Deal. 

As basis for the discussion, participants will exchange on their knowledge and understanding of:

a)    Existing conceptions of a Post-Growth European Green Deal grounded in Degrowth research

b)    Community-led initiatives as change agents within the degrowth movement working from the bottom-up

2.3 Co-Creation workshop: Bringing the Time for Collective Action Manifesto to life
Participants will form groups of 5-6 people and will be asked to create an action plan to support the implementation of the key demands of the Time for Collective Manifesto. Hereby, the participants are invited to draw upon their insights from their degrowth research to support, complement or critique policy recommendations from the manifesto.

At the level of the head, we will ask participants to reflect on what they think about the thesis and which data, facts, and knowledge are relevant to Time for Collective Action Manifesto
At the level of the heart, participants will be asked to reflect on how the Time for Collective Action Manifesto makes them feel and what values are reflected in these feelings.

Finally, with regards to the hands, participants are invited to which concrete action they will take within their communities to work towards a post-growth European Green Deal with communities at the heart.

III. Follow-up & Outputs
Participants will be encouraged to share their action plans and collaboration ideas and to sign up to become a campaign partner for the Time for Collective Action Manifesto.

IV. Resources
https://communitiesforfuture.org/policy-and-advocacy/time-for-collective-action-manifesto/
