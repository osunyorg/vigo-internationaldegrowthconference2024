Beyond words: embodied, situated and transdisciplinary approaches in participatory research summary: >-

This interactive session aims to reflect on the use of visual languages —in a broader sense— in both collective art-science research and situated collaborative processes. We seek to bring together a diverse group of feminist researchers and practitioners to dialogue around the ways we all develop embodied research practices.— departing from the reflection on the ways (our) bodies and emotions are involved when we do research. This encounter will be developed in two sections. In the first one,organizers will share previous science and practice-based projects engaged with a diversity of socio-ecological issues and embodied research practices with a focus on the use of visual languages. In the second section, we will facilitate a collective mapping exercise around the issues portrayed in the projects and the role of diverse languages in arts-science research. For this discussion, one of the organizers, Carla Boserman, will draw the dialogue through graphical listening practice.

Full description

This interactive session will engage with visual languages as a
form of embodied research to critically explore how they are used and
contribute to arts-science research processes and wider social-ecological
transformations. The session will reflect on the experiences, dynamics,
frictions, challenges and possibilities of practicing embodied research.
Embodied research acknowledges the lived and embodied experience of the
researcher and the researched, along with the feelings, sensations, and
emotions that arise from the research experience. Embodied research comes into
play when we engage with arts-based practices as part of the research
processes, such as when using storytelling, performance or focusing on
practices enacted by particular individuals or groups.  The session will focus on the role of
embodied and situated research, with particular attention to the visual arts as
a research tool that can open the space to other languages and ways of knowing
and relating to others, including other-than-humans. It will do so by bringing
together a diverse group of feminist researchers and practitioners and
establishing a dialogue with other participants in the session. Our motivation
to propose this interactive session responds to an ongoing conversation and a collective
interest in understanding in which ways our arts-based approaches, experiences
and methodologies intersect and the ways this can nurture and broaden our own
processes.  It also responds to an
increasing interest in the ecological economics and degrowth communities to
engage with arts-based approaches and the possibilities that the arts bring to
mobilize spaces often difficult to access with traditional methodologies, such
as the emotions and feelings linked to environmental and social conflicts. It is well established that arts can provide a space for
imagination, experimentation, and rehearsing alternative worlds and
relationships. In this regard, arts can enable the emergence of other values
and relational practices  that can help
transform hegemonic imaginaries. Moreover, involving our bodies in the process
of knowledge production opens the floor to other beyond-words forms of
expressing and knowing, often marginalized in academic settings.  Attention to different languages of valuation
and subaltern voices and experiences is both at the root of ecological
economics and the transformations required towards degrowth. The dialogues and
outcomes from this session aim to contribute to the growing interest that both
epistemic communities have on methodologies that foster radical and critical
thinking.  The 90 min session will be structured in two parts. During the
first part , we will share audio-visual stories, drawings and other material
from a selection of arts-science and practice-based research projects carried
out by the organizers. Some of them are Shared
Dialogues and Echoes, absences, porosities and other forms of
(re)encounter through the water . These projects will be introduced
at the beginning of the event. During the second part of the session, we will display and share
materials from each project and give participants the opportunity to explore
them. These materials will be the starting point for the second part of the
session, which will take place in the format of a collective reflection and a
live mapping space drawing on visual material, questions and conclusions from
the session. This second  part of the
session will be structured around the following core questions:
What reflections and emotions do the
materials generate?
What
strategies can we articulate to strengthen and incentivize arts-science
collaborations?

How can these
approaches activate social transformation processes?
Throughout the session
Carla Boserman (co-organizer) will perform a graphic listening and reporting
that will be made available to all participants and conference organizers
afterwards.

Our motivation to propose this interactive session responds to an ongoing conversation and a collective interest in understanding in which ways our arts-based approaches, experiences and methodologies intersect and the ways this can nurture and broaden our own processes.  It also responds to an increasing interest in the ecological economics and degrowth communities to engage with arts-based approaches and the possibilities that the arts bring to mobilize spaces often difficult to access with traditional methodologies, such as the emotions and feelings linked to environmental and social conflicts.

Moreover, involving our bodies in the process of knowledge production opens the floor to other beyond-words forms of expressing and knowing, often marginalized in academic settings.  Attention to different languages of valuation and subaltern voices and experiences is both at the root of ecological economics and the transformations required towards degrowth. The dialogues and outcomes from this session aim to contribute to the growing interest that both epistemic communities have on methodologies that foster radical and critical thinking. 

The 90 min session will be structured in two parts. During the first part, we will share audio-visual stories, drawings and other material from a selection of arts-science and practice-based research projects carried out by the organizers. Some of them are Shared Dialogues and Echoes, absences, porosities and other forms of (re)encounter through the water. These projects will be introduced at the beginning of the event.

During the second part of the session, we will display and share materials from each project and give participants the opportunity to explore them. These materials will be the starting point for the second part of the session, which will take place in the format of a collective reflection and a live mapping space drawing on visual material, questions and conclusions from the session. This second  part of the session will be structured around the following core questions:

  1. What reflections and emotions do the materials generate?
  2. What strategies can we articulate to strengthen and incentivize arts-science collaborations?
  3. How can these approaches activate social transformation processes?

Throughout the session Carla Boserman (co-organizer) will perform a graphic listening and reporting that will be made available to all participants and conference organizers afterwards.
