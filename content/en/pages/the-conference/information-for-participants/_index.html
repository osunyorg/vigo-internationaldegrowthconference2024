  • The Opening Session will be held at the Pazo da Cultura of Pontevedra on Tuesday 18 June 2024 from 16.00h (CEST time). More information can be found here. 
  • To attend the activities of the conference it will be necessary to register at the points and times provided for this purpose, which will be:
    · Pazo da Cultura (main hall) - Tuesday 18 June, 13.30-16.00h
    · Escola de Enxeñería Forestal (‘soto’ hall) - Monday 17 June, 09.00-11.00h; Wednesday 19 June, 08.30-11.00h & 13.30-15.30h; Thursday 20 June, 08.30-11.00 & 13.30-15.30h; Friday 21 June, ask at the information desk.
  • When you register you will receive a recycled paper bag containing: meal tickets (if you have previously booked them); a postcard with QR codes to access the most relevant information; several identification stickers to use throughout the conference, on which you must write your name and the name of your organisation and stick them on your clothes in a visible place. 
  • Information about the conference programme (sessions, times and locations) can be found at this link.
  • During the whole week, we will also count on many non-academic activities open to the public (no registration is required). Some will be developed in English and others in Spanish/Galician. All the information about these activities can be found here. 
  • There will be a permanent information desk in the Escola de Enxeñería Forestal from Wednesday 19 to Friday 21 June at soto floor.
  • Several volunteers will be available at the event venues. They will wear a blue T-shirt with the conference logo so that they can be easily identified.
  • The lunches will be only for those who have previously booked them. They must be handed over to the persons in charge before access to the meal area. Due to high demand, no tickets will be available for sale on-site during the conference. 
    For those with no booked meal, there is a cafeteria in the Escola de Enxeñería Forestal, and also in the Facultade de Comunicación (a bigger one). Out of the campus you can find two supermarkets: Mercadona and Froiz, both less than 15 minutes walking.
  • There will be several free-use rooms at the Escola de Enxeñería Forestal and Pazo da Cultura. Please, ask the volunteer team to use them if needed.

  • Presenters for track sessions (starting with T.) and special open sessions (starting with SS) will have a maximum of 15 minutes to make their presentation. This does not apply to artistic, interactive or closed special sessions (in this case, please contact with the organisers for specific details).
  • Presenters should bring their presentation on a USB flash drive and download it to the computer in the room. No external computers can be connected, the computer in the room must be used. A volunteer team member will be available to assist participants with these questions. 
  • Presenters must be in the room for their session 10 minutes before the start of the session.
  • The order of presentations within each session may be agreed between the presenters and the chairs prior to the start of the session.
  • Each session has an assigned chair. If for any reason the chair is not present, the person presenting last (or another of the presenters who wishes to do so) will become chair of the session.
  • Some modifications have been made to the programme in recent weeks to try to meet the needs of some of the speakers. Please check the programme before your session to make sure you are clear about the day and time.
  • We encourage speakers to use the official conference template and typography for your presentation. They can be downloaded at the bottom of this page.
notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- For chairs slug: "for-chairs" position: 2 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • The main functions of the chairs will be:
    - To agree the order of presentations with the speakers
    - To ensure that speakers do not exceed the maximum time allotted for each presentation
    - To introduce the different presentations
    - To open and moderate the time for questions and answers.
  • There will be coloured cards in each room that chairs can use if they wish to indicate to the speakers how much time they have left: green (more than 5 minutes available); orange (less than 3 minutes available); red (less than 1 minute available).
  • Chairs should be in the room for their session at least 15 minutes before the start of the session.
  • They should ensure that speakers download their presentations to room computer before the start of the session.
  • They will agree the order of the presentations with the speakers before the start of the session.
  • Some modifications have been made to the programme in recent weeks to try to meet the needs of some of the speakers. Please check the programme before your session to make sure you are clear about the day and time.
  • A volunteer team member will always be available to provide support if needed. If he/she is not in the room, please look for him/her in the adjoining rooms.
  • If changes are made to the room configuration, the furniture should be rearranged at the end of the session so that it is as it was previously.

  • Organisers should be in the room for their session at least 15 minutes before the start of the session.
  • A volunteer team member will always be available to provide support if needed. If he/she is not in the room, please look for him/her in the adjoining rooms. Incidents should be reported to these persons, who will try to resolve them or, if this is not possible, they will contact the conference organisers.
  • Presenters should download their presentation to the room computer. No external computers can be connected (with a few exceptions), the computer in the room must be used. A volunteer team member will be available to assist participants with these questions.
  • For remote participation, organisers should arrange for this to be possible. The organisation will not provide additional equipment or support for these issues.
  • If changes are made to the room configuration, the furniture should be rearranged at the end of the session so that it is as it was previously.
notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- For poster presenters slug: "for-poster-presenters" position: 4 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • Each author must bring her/his printed poster.
  • Since thinking outside of conventional boundaries is an important theme for this conference, we encourage participants to be creative in preparing the posters, without leaving aside rigor. Aim to explain the work effectively and dynamically to capture public imaginations.
  • Posters must be in vertical format, with dimensions between 70-90cm wide x 100-120cm high.
  • A digital version of the poster must be sent before 11 June 2024 at 23.59 hours to review_esee-degrowth2024@uvigo.gal. These images will be uploaded to a gallery on the conference website.
  • Before the start of the conference, authors will receive additional information on how, when, and where to place their posters.
  • Each author will have an assigned place for her/his posters. The days and times for the placement of posters will be: 
    - Wednesday: 9:30 - 10:30 or 13:30 a 14:30
    - Thursday: 14:00 a 14:30
    A volunteer team member will be there in these slots to provide material and help authors if needed. Outside these hours, material should be requested at the information desk.
  • Authors will have the opportunity to explain their work and answer questions from the audience during the posters sessions on Wednesday 19 (Posters session I), Thursday 20 (Posters session II) and Friday 21 (Posters session III) from 14.30 to 15.00h. Authors are free to be with their posters on these days and times for as long as they wish.
  • The list of posters can be found in the Posters session I.
